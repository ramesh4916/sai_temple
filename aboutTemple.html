About Temple

Shri Shirdi Sai Baba Temple in Isnapur, Telangana, is a place of spiritual significance. The temple is dedicated to Shirdi Sai Baba, a revered spiritual master who lived in Shirdi, Maharashtra, during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. It stands as a symbol of faith and devotion for countless devotees.

Located on NH 65, near Lakdaram Gate, this temple is easily accessible by road and public transportation. It was constructed in the early 1980s by a group of devout followers of Shirdi Sai Baba. The temple complex is extensive, featuring a main shrine, a spacious prayer hall, a dedicated dining hall, and a serene garden.

The main shrine houses a beautiful statue of Shirdi Sai Baba, adorned with fresh flowers and garlands. The prayer hall is a place where devotees gather to sing hymns, chant prayers, and partake in spiritual discourses. The dining hall serves daily meals to devotees, embodying the spirit of selfless service.

Shri Shirdi Sai Baba Temple welcomes individuals of all castes, religions, and creeds. It is a place of solace and spirituality, where devotees seek the blessings of Shirdi Sai Baba. Thursdays, considered Sai Baba's day, witness a surge of devotees seeking his divine grace.

The temple conducts various religious activities, including aarti, bhajans, and kirtans. Additionally, a library within the temple premises offers a repository of knowledge about Shirdi Sai Baba and his teachings.

Visiting the Sri Shirdi Sai Baba Temple in Isnapur offers a range of experiences:

  • Offer prayers and seek blessings from Shirdi Sai Baba.
  • Participate in aarti, bhajans, and kirtans.
  • Explore the temple library and delve into the teachings of Shirdi Sai Baba.
  • Engage in religious discourses and temple events.
  • Contribute your time as a volunteer to support the temple's activities.
  • Make donations to aid the temple's growth and maintenance.

The Shri Shirdi Sai Baba Temple warmly welcomes all individuals, providing a space for inner peace, spiritual guidance, and personal growth.
